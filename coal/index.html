How this model works

Every number here is traced back to a public government or NGO dataset, joined on the mine's federal MSHA ID. Where methane is not directly measured we estimate it, but we always record how strong the evidence is and carry that uncertainty through to every plant. This guide explains the evidence tiers, how coal is tracked from mine to smokestack, and how the pieces are cross-checked.

A Methane evidence tiers

Each mine's methane is tagged with the strongest evidence available. The table shows how many mines fall in each tier, and — because a handful of gassy underground mines dominate — how much of the national net methane each tier accounts for.

  • Measured — operator-measured underground ventilation & degasification CH₄ reported to EPA GHGRP Subpart FF. Strongest evidence; used whenever it exists.
  • Reported — a mine-specific liberation figure from the Global Coal Mine Tracker where no GHGRP measurement is available.
  • Modeled (depth) — no direct report; liberated CH₄ estimated from seam depth using EPA GHGI basin gas-content curves.
  • Default factor — neither depth nor report; a basin / coal-rank default emission factor (surface vs. underground).

B Tracking coal shipments

To power plants (observed). EIA-923's fuel-receipts schedule reports every coal delivery to a US power plant, tagged with the supplying mine's MSHA ID. We join on that ID, so 99%+ of power-sector coal is matched mine→plant with real reported tonnage. These flows are drawn as solid ribbons/arcs.

Non-power (modeled). There is no public mine-level receipt data for exports, coke/steel, or industrial coal. We take each mine's total production and allocate the non-power remainder across sectors using the EIA Annual Coal Distribution Report's state×sector shares. Exports are split to terminals by origin-region routing so the terminal totals match EIA customs-district export data. These flows are modeled — drawn dotted and flagged throughout.

C Tracking consumption

Power plants. EIA-923 also reports what each plant actually burned — both tonnage and energy (MMBtu). Heat rate (Btu/kWh) and implied efficiency come from consumption ÷ net generation, and combustion CO₂ from heat rate × an EPA rank-based emission factor. We show coal consumed next to coal received so stockpile draw-down or build-up is visible.

Export terminals. Terminal throughput is anchored to EIA quarterly coal-export tonnage by customs district. The terminal locations are real ports; the specific mine→terminal assignment is modeled, not observed.

D Cross-checks & verification

  • Measured beats reported. Where both GHGRP and GCMT exist we use the measurement and flag big disagreements. Example: GCMT's reported figure for Lively Grove implied 458 kt CH₄; the GHGRP measurement is 3.4 kt — a 135× overstatement we discarded.
  • National reconciliation. Summed mine-level net CH₄ (1.73 Mt) is checked against EPA's national GHGI coal total (~1.84 Mt); the GHGRP-covered subset (1.155 Mt) matches EPA FLIGHT to ~0.3%.
  • Receipts vs. burn. For every plant we compare documented shipments to reported consumption as an internal consistency check (shown on each plant's panel).
  • Automated guards. Before any build can publish, hard checks reject impossible methane intensities, coordinates outside the US, net > gross, and MSHA join rates below 85%.

Sources: EIA-923 & EIA-860, EIA Annual Coal Distribution Report, EIA coal-export data, EPA GHGRP Subpart FF, EPA GHGI Annex 3.4, and the Global Coal Mine Tracker. Modeled allocations are labeled as such wherever they appear.
